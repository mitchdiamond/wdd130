My Favorite City

Laie, Hawaii is a serene coastal town on Oahu’s North Shore, renowned for its rich cultural heritage and spiritual significance. It’s home to the Polynesian Cultural Center, a vibrant attraction that celebrates the traditions of Pacific Island nations through immersive village experiences and performances. Laie also hosts the historic Laie Hawaii Temple, the first temple of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints built outside the continental U.S., adding to its reputation as a spiritual hub. Surrounded by lush landscapes and dramatic ocean views—especially from Laie Point—this small town blends natural beauty with deep-rooted traditions, making it a unique and peaceful destination.
