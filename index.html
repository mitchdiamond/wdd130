Utah

Utah is a landlocked state in the Mountain West region known for its stunning natural landscapes, including Arches National Park, Zion National Park, and the Great Salt Lake. Historically, the state was inhabited by various Native American groups before becoming a destination for Mormon pioneers in 1847, eventually leading to its statehood in 1896. Today, Utah has a diverse economy and a population of over 3 million, with the majority living along the Wasatch Front.
